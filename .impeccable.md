# AutoReception — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Small home-services business owners: HVAC technicians, plumbers, electricians, and roofers. They are time-poor, skeptical of tech products, and often checking the site on a phone between jobs. They don't care about "beautiful design" — they care whether it solves a real problem (missed calls = lost money) quickly and without friction. The job to be done is: understand in 10 seconds whether this will make them more money, sign up, and get back to work.

### Brand Personality
**Reliable. Fast. No-BS.**

Tradesperson-to-tradesperson directness — no corporate fluff, no startup cheerfulness. AutoReception should feel like a tool built by someone who understands the trades, not adapted from a generic SaaS template. The tone is confident and matter-of-fact: "This works. Here's how. Start now."

Emotional goals: confidence that it will just work, urgency to act (every missed call costs money), and zero buyer anxiety (flat price, no surprises).

### Aesthetic Direction
**Reference**: Vercel.com — dark, sharp, high-contrast, clean typography. Decisive whitespace. Typography doing most of the visual work. No decorative complexity.

**Anti-reference**: The generic SaaS template aesthetic — boilerplate gradients, placeholder logos, "we just shipped it" energy. Any design decision that reads as "starter kit".

**Theme**: Dark mode only. Near-black backgrounds (`#0f0f12` family), pure white body text, a single strong accent color used with intention.

**Gradient intent**: The cyan→magenta→orange animated border is from the starter template and should be replaced. Instead: a single accent — **amber/orange** (`#F98324` or similar) — that speaks to trades (safety, energy, urgency) and creates tight visual hierarchy. The spinning border effect can stay for the primary CTA only, but using a constrained amber-to-orange palette rather than a rainbow.

**Accent strategy**:
- Primary CTA: amber-orange (`#F98324`) — action, urgency
- Positive/checkmarks: emerald-400 — kept as-is, works well
- Warnings/limits: amber-200/600 — kept as-is, works well
- Focus rings: amber or white, replacing the current pink-500

**Typography**: Montserrat Alternates for headings/buttons (already configured), Montserrat for body. Tight tracking on uppercase labels. Gradient text on hero h1 only (white → neutral-400 — keep this, it's sharp).

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness.** Every component should communicate its purpose at a glance. Remove decorative elements that don't earn their space. A busy page signals "generic template" — the exact anti-reference.

2. **Dark and decisive.** The near-black palette with sharp white text signals a professional-grade tool, not a consumer app. Think Vercel dashboard energy: confident, not cozy.

3. **Trades-first, always.** Before shipping a design decision, ask: would a plumber on a job site understand this immediately? Language, iconography, and layout should feel built for them, not adapted from a tech startup.

4. **One accent, used with intention.** Amber-orange is the single accent color. It does all the heavy lifting for CTAs, highlights, and urgency signals. Multi-color gradients dilute this — resist adding secondary accent colors.

5. **Typography carries the weight.** Copy like "Every missed call is a $500 job walking to your competitor" is more powerful than any illustration. Let strong typographic hierarchy do the visual work before reaching for decorative elements.
